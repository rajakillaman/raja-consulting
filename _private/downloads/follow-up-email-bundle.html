Email 01 / 04
Same-Day Inquiry Reply
When to send: Within 2–4 hours of receiving an inquiry — same day at the latest. Speed is the single biggest driver of conversion at this stage. The goal is to feel warm, personal, and immediately useful while setting up the next step.
Subject Line Options (choose one)
Re: Your [Wedding / Event] Inquiry — I'd love to connect
Your [Wedding Date] — a few quick thoughts from me
Thank you for reaching out — let's find time to talk

To:[Client First Name] <[client@email.com]>
From:[Your Name] · [Your Business Name]
Subject:Re: Your [Wedding / Event] Inquiry — I'd love to connect
Hi [First Name], Thank you so much for reaching out — I'm genuinely excited about your [wedding / event] on [date or "your upcoming date"]. From what you've shared, this sounds like exactly the kind of [celebration / event] I love working on. [Add one specific, genuine detail from their inquiry — e.g., "A garden ceremony with intimate dining sounds absolutely beautiful."] Here's what I'd suggest as a next step: let's hop on a quick 20-minute call so I can learn more about your vision and share how I can support you. There's no commitment — just a chance to see if we're a good fit. [Booking link or: "You can reply here with a few times that work for you and I'll make it happen."] In the meantime, feel free to browse [your portfolio / our work / recent events] at [your website]. Looking forward to speaking soon. Warmly, [Your Name] [Your Title] · [Business Name] [Phone] · [Website]
Tips for this email
  • Reference something specific from their inquiry — even one line makes it feel personal and not templated.
  • Keep it under 150 words. Long emails at this stage overwhelm leads and reduce reply rates.
  • Always include a clear single CTA — one link, one ask. Don't offer multiple options.
  • If you don't hear back in 48 hours, move to Email 03 (Reactivation).
Email 02 / 04
Post-Tour / Post-Call Recap
When to send: Within 1–2 hours of ending a venue tour or discovery call. Striking while the emotional energy is still fresh dramatically increases the chance they'll take the next step. This email serves as both a recap and a soft push toward commitment.
Subject Line Options (choose one)
So great meeting you — here's what we covered
Your [wedding / event] — next steps after our call
Following up from today's [tour / call] + a few things to share

To:[Client First Name(s)]
From:[Your Name] · [Your Business Name]
Subject:So great meeting you — here's what we covered
Hi [First Name(s)], It was so great [meeting you today / speaking with you this afternoon] — I really enjoyed learning more about your vision for [the wedding / your event / the day]. Here's a quick recap of what we covered: • [Key point 1 from your conversation — e.g., "You're looking at an outdoor ceremony with indoor dining for ~120 guests."] • [Key point 2 — e.g., "Your ideal budget range is $X–$X and you'd like to keep florals in-house."] • [Key point 3 — e.g., "Your date is flexible, but you're leaning toward late September or early October."] Based on everything you shared, I think the [package name / service tier] would be the strongest fit for you — it covers [briefly explain why it fits their specific situation]. Here's what happens next: [Describe the next step clearly — e.g., "I'll send over a formal proposal within 24 hours." or "To hold your date, I'll need a signed agreement and deposit."] Any questions before then — just reply here. I'm happy to jump back on a quick call too. Talk soon, [Your Name] [Your Title] · [Business Name] [Phone] · [Website]
Tips for this email
  • The recap bullet points show you were listening — this builds trust faster than any sales pitch.
  • Gently recommend a specific package rather than listing all your options. Fewer choices = faster decisions.
  • Make the "next step" extremely clear and single-action. Ambiguity causes stalls.
  • If they don't respond in 5–7 days, move to Email 03 (Reactivation).
Email 03 / 04
No-Response Reactivation
When to send: 7–14 days after your last outreach with no reply. Most leads don't ghost because they're not interested — they get busy. This email is low-pressure, adds value, and gives them an easy "in" to re-engage without embarrassment.
Subject Line Options (choose one)
Still thinking about your [wedding / event] — wanted to check in
Just leaving this here in case the timing is better now
Quick note from [Your Name]

To:[Client First Name]
From:[Your Name] · [Your Business Name]
Subject:Still thinking about your [wedding / event] — wanted to check in
Hi [First Name], I know how busy things can get — no pressure at all, just wanted to follow up in case this got buried or the timing wasn't quite right. I'm still thinking about [the detail you mentioned / their event / their vision] and would genuinely love the chance to support you if it's a good fit. To make it easy, here's a quick overview of how working with me looks: → [One-sentence summary of your primary service / value prop] → [One concrete outcome or benefit — e.g., "Most clients tell me the biggest relief is not having to manage vendor communication themselves."] → [Social proof or credibility note — e.g., "I've coordinated 40+ weddings in the [area] area over the past 3 years."] If you're still exploring options, I'm happy to answer any questions — no commitment needed. And if you've moved in a different direction, no worries at all — I just didn't want to leave you without a proper follow-up in case I can still help. Either way, wishing you the best with the planning! [Your Name] [Your Title] · [Business Name] [Phone] · [Website]
Tips for this email
  • The "no worries if you've moved on" line paradoxically increases replies — it removes pressure and feels genuine.
  • Keep the tone warm, not desperate. You're checking in as a professional, not chasing.
  • Adding a new piece of value (a stat, a quick tip, a social proof point) gives them a reason to open the email.
  • This is typically the last follow-up. If no response after this, remove them from active outreach.
Email 04 / 04
Booking Urgency Close
When to send: When a prospect has expressed strong interest or received a proposal but hasn't signed yet. Use only when urgency is real (e.g., their date is approaching, a competing inquiry is pending, or your availability is genuinely limited). Manufactured urgency loses trust fast.
Subject Line Options (choose one)
Quick update about [their date] availability
Wanted to give you a heads up before I respond to another inquiry
Last chance to hold [their date] — a note from [Your Name]

To:[Client First Name(s)]
From:[Your Name] · [Your Business Name]
Subject:Quick update about [their date] availability
Hi [First Name(s)], I wanted to reach out directly before responding to another inquiry that came in for [their date or date range]. I hold only one [wedding / event] per date to make sure every couple gets my full attention — which means I can only commit your date once I receive a signed agreement and retainer. I don't want to have to turn you away if someone else books first, so I wanted to give you the first opportunity. If you're ready to move forward, here's the link to review and sign the agreement: [Contract / proposal link] The retainer to hold your date is [$XXX] and can be paid via [payment methods]. If the timing still isn't right or you have questions you'd like to talk through first, just let me know — I'm happy to set up a quick call this week. [Your Name] [Your Title] · [Business Name] [Phone] · [Website] P.S. [Optional: Add one genuine reason you're excited about their event — it softens the urgency and makes it feel personal, not pushy.]
Tips for this email
  • Only use this when urgency is real. If you fabricate a competing inquiry and they find out, the relationship is gone.
  • Frame it as doing them a favor by giving them first right of refusal — because you are.
  • Include the contract or proposal link directly in the email. Every extra step they have to take reduces conversion.
  • The P.S. line is highly read — use it for something genuine and specific to them, not generic.
