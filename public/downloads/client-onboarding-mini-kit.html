Document 03 / 04
Kickoff Call Agenda
How to use: Use this as your internal guide during the call. You can share your screen with the agenda visible so clients feel organized and respected. Adjust time blocks based on your actual call length (this template is built for 60 minutes; scale down proportionally for shorter calls).
Client[Client Name(s)]
Date[Call Date]
Format60-minute video / phone call
0–5 min
Welcome & Introductions
Thank them for booking. Share a brief intro about yourself and your background. Ask them to share one thing they're most excited about for the event — starts the energy on a high note.
5–20 min
Review Intake Form & Fill Gaps
Walk through the key points from their intake form. Clarify anything that wasn't clear. Ask follow-up questions on vision, priorities, and any concerns they flagged. Let them do most of the talking — listen for the emotion behind their answers.
20–35 min
Budget & Vendor Status Review
Confirm current budget and where the biggest unknowns are. Review which vendors are booked and which still need to be sourced. Flag any timeline concerns (e.g., popular vendors book out 12–18 months). Introduce your vendor recommendation process.
35–48 min
Project Timeline & Milestones
Walk through the key milestones from today to the event date. Confirm which decisions need to be made first. Clarify your role at each stage. Set expectations around how long vendor sourcing and responses typically take.
48–56 min
Working Preferences & Communication Norms
Confirm their preferred communication method and frequency. Walk them through your tools (shared folder, project tracker, etc.). Set response time expectations clearly for both parties. Address any questions about the scope of your service agreement.
56–60 min
Wrap-Up & Clear Next Actions
Summarize the top 3 action items coming out of this call:
  → Client will: [e.g., confirm venue point of contact, share mood board links]
  → You will: [e.g., send vendor shortlist by Friday, share project tracker link]
  → Next check-in: [e.g., 2-week follow-up call, or "I'll email you in 5 business days"]

End on an enthusiastic note. This is their last impression of the call.
Tips for this call
  • Send the agenda to clients 24 hours before the call — it makes you look organized and helps them prepare too.
  • Always end with 2–3 specific action items. Vague endings ("I'll be in touch!") create confusion and delayed follow-up.
  • Record the call (with permission) so you can reference it when building the project plan later.
Document 04 / 04
Expectations Checklist
When to share: Send this as an attachment after the kickoff call, or during the call as a walkthrough. This is your scope-setting document — it prevents the most common sources of friction in client relationships by making expectations explicit from day one. Ask clients to review and confirm they've read it.
Client[Client Name(s)]
Planner[Your Name]
Event Date[Date]
Communication
  • All planning communication goes through email ([your email]) to maintain a clear record.
    Not text, not Instagram DMs, not voicemail — email only for planning decisions.
  • Response time is within [X business hours/days] on weekdays for both parties.
    Requests sent after 6pm or on weekends will be addressed the next business day.
  • Day-of urgent contact: text [phone number] — for the event day only.
  • Major decisions (venue changes, vendor swaps, budget adjustments) require written confirmation from both parties before action is taken.
Scope of Services
  • Services included in your package are listed in your signed service agreement. Anything outside the agreement scope will be quoted separately before work begins.
  • Vendor payments and contracts are handled directly between you and each vendor — we do not collect or hold vendor funds.
    We will help you track contracts and manage communication, but financial transactions are between you and your vendors.
  • We will source vendor recommendations based on your brief, but the final selection decision is always yours.
  • Significant scope changes (e.g., adding an event day, expanding guest count by 30%+) may require a scope revision and adjusted fee.
Timeline & Approvals
  • A working timeline will be shared no later than [X weeks] before the event. Your approval is required within [X days].
  • Vendor contracts should be shared with us within [X days] of signing so we can add them to the coordination file.
  • Final guest count must be confirmed no later than [X weeks] before the event for venue and catering accuracy.
  • Design and décor decisions should be finalized [X weeks] prior to the event to allow adequate setup preparation.
Day-Of Logistics
  • We will be your primary point of contact for all vendors on the event day. Avoid directing vendors directly during the event — let us handle it so you can enjoy the day.
  • A finalized timeline and vendor contact sheet will be distributed to all vendors [X days] before the event.
  • We will conduct a venue walkthrough [X days or weeks] before the event. At least one member of your party is welcome to join.
  • Guests should be directed to a designated family/wedding party point of contact for logistics questions on the day — not to us directly.
Cancellations & Changes
  • Cancellation and refund terms are outlined in your signed service agreement. Please review them before this call.
  • Date changes are subject to availability. We cannot guarantee availability for a new date at the same rate.
  • We strongly recommend purchasing event insurance. This protects both parties in the case of unforeseen circumstances.
Confirmation
By proceeding with this engagement, both parties confirm they have read and understand the above expectations. These do not replace the service agreement — they supplement it for clarity and a smoother working relationship.
